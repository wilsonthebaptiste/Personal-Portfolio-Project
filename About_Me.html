About Me

Hi, I'm Wilson Jean-Baptiste, a dedicated and innovative chemical engineer passionate about advancing sustainable technology and safety in engineering practices. With a solid foundation from Howard University, where I graduated Magna Cum Laude in Chemical Engineering and was an active member of the Omega Chi Epsilon Honor Society, my academic journey has been marked by a commitment to excellence and a deep curiosity for the intersection of science and technology.

"Life is full of pain, but the beauty of it is we have the power to choose our pains. Minimize your regrets"
My Experience
  • The start of my professional journey began by applying theoretical concepts to real-world applications through an impactful internship at NASA-JPL. There, I engaged in pioneering research, investigating the interfacial behavior of amino acids on gold surfaces.
  • The next step in my journey led me to an internship at NASA-Goddard where I worked on building a database of potential surface materials on the icy moon, Ceres. This was also during the lockdown period and it resulted in a lot of personal development navigating through such unique circumstances.
  • My first "true" career experience was as a Process Engineer at Plug Power where my role centered around optimizing hydrogen fuel distribution systems through meticulous design and standardization of process instrumentation diagrams. My contributions supported initiatives to make hydrogen fuel more accessible and safe for users.
